Executive Summary
Fresco Enterprises manages 10 TB of data with mixed access patterns, currently stored at a flat Standard tier regardless of access frequency. This document recommends a three-bucket tiered S3 architecture that reduces annual storage costs by approximately 25% while maintaining 99.99% availability for active data and satisfying 7-year retention requirements through Glacier Deep Archive with Object Lock.
Problem Statement
All 10 TB — including 2 TB of archival data accessed less than once per year — is stored at Standard pricing ($0.023/GB-mo). This results in significant overspend on cold data that does not require immediate retrieval. A tiered approach aligned to actual access behavior eliminates this waste without affecting operational availability.

Proposed Architecture
Three dedicated S3 buckets, each with a lifecycle policy matched to its workload.
Bucket Size Storage Class Purpose
active-data 8 TB Standard → IA (day 91+) Project files, reports, intelligence products
archive-data 2 TB Glacier Deep Archive Historical records, completed project archives

Design Decisions
01
Active Data Lifecycle
Standard → Standard-IA after 90 days
Defense project deliverables see the heaviest access in the first 90 days — review cycles, revisions, submissions. After that window, access drops sharply. The 90-day threshold aligns the transition to observed behavior, avoiding IA retrieval fees during the active phase. Validate with S3 Analytics before production deployment.
02
Archive Storage Class
Glacier Deep Archive
At $0.00099/GB-mo, Glacier Deep Archive is the lowest-cost S3 storage tier. 12–48 hour bulk retrieval is acceptable for historical records accessed less than once per year. Cost is 95% less than Standard.
03
Encryption
SSE-KMS on all buckets
Satisfies NIST 800-53 SC-28. Supports key rotation and provides a full audit trail via CloudTrail for every encryption and decryption event.
04
Object Lock — Archive Bucket
Enabled · Compliance Mode · 7-year retention
Compliance mode prevents deletion by any user, including the root account. This is a one-way door — once set it cannot be shortened — making it appropriate for regulatory retention. Governance mode is insufficient for this use case.
05
Versioning
Enabled on active-data and archive-data
Protects against accidental deletion and overwrites. Old versions on active-data transition to IA after 30 days to contain versioning overhead costs.
06
Public Access
Blocked on all buckets
Mandatory for defense contractor environments. Enforced at the bucket and account level.
